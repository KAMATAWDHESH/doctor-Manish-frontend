Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ's)

Orthopedics is a branch of medicine that focuses on the care of the musculoskeletal system. This includes bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, muscles, and nerves. Orthopedic doctors diagnose and treat various conditions including injuries, degenerative diseases, infections, tumors, and congenital disorders affecting the musculoskeletal system.

Dr. Manish Ranjan is widely recognized as one of the best orthopedic doctors in Patna. With over 17 years of experience, he specializes in joint replacement surgeries, trauma surgery, arthritis treatment, and sports injury management. He has successfully completed over 5000+ orthopedic procedures.

An orthopedic doctor (orthopedist) is a physician who specializes in diagnosing, treating, preventing, and rehabilitating injuries and diseases of the musculoskeletal system. They perform surgeries like joint replacements, arthroscopy, fracture repairs, and provide non-surgical treatments such as medications, physical therapy, and injections for various bone and joint conditions.

Orthopaedic treatment refers to the medical care provided for conditions affecting the musculoskeletal system. Treatment options include non-surgical methods like medication, physical therapy, braces, and injections, as well as surgical procedures such as joint replacement, arthroscopy, fracture fixation, and ligament reconstruction. The treatment approach depends on the specific condition, severity, and patient needs.

Orthopedics includes diagnosis and treatment of conditions affecting bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, muscles, and nerves. This encompasses areas such as spine care, sports medicine, joint replacement, trauma surgery, arthritis management, hand surgery, foot and ankle surgery, pediatric orthopedics, and rehabilitation services.

The most common orthopedic procedures include knee replacement surgery, hip replacement surgery, ACL reconstruction, arthroscopy surgery, fracture repairs, spinal fusion, carpal tunnel release, rotator cuff repair, and joint arthroscopy.
